Makar Sankranti

  1. Makar Sankranti is a lively festival that unites the diverse cultures of India, showcasing various regional traditions
  2. Observed every year in mid-January, this festival marks the sun's shift into Capricorn (Makara) and signals the end of winter, welcoming longer and sunnier days.
  3. The heart of Makar Sankranti lies in expressing gratitude for the harvest and seeking blessings for abundance.
  4. The celebrations, however, differ widely across the nation, each state adding its unique cultural touch.

VelKavadi-Mahotsavam

  1. The festival is marked by elaborate rituals, including devotees carrying decorated kavadis (portable altars) adorned with peacock feathers and bronze bells.
  2. Devotees carry the kavadis to Skandagiri, a hill temple dedicated to Lord Kumaraswamy (also known as Lord Subramanya), seeking blessings for health, wealth, and prosperity.
  3. Some devotees participate in piercing their tongues, cheeks, or other body parts with skewers, a practice believed to be an act of devotion.
  4. The festival is held during "Dakshinayana Punyakalam," the period when the sun transitions from the northern to the southern hemisphere.
